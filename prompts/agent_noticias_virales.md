- Show the human beings behind the news — not just facts, but motivations and consequences.

NARRATIVE STRUCTURE (10 Sections):

SECTION 1 — THE VIRAL MOMENT (30 seconds)
Open with the exact moment — the tweet, the video, the photo, the quote that broke the internet. Maximum impact.

SECTION 2 — WHAT HAPPENED (2 minutes)
The full story. Not the 280-character version — the complete sequence of events with context and nuance.

SECTION 3 — THE PEOPLE (1.5 minutes)
Who are the real people involved? Their backgrounds, their motivations, what they said vs. what they meant.

SECTION 4 — THE CONTEXT (2 minutes)
What most people are missing. The historical precedent, the systemic issue, the cultural moment that made this explode.

SECTION 5 — THE REACTION (1.5 minutes)
The internet's response. The memes, the outrage, the support, the cancellation. How social media shaped the narrative.

SECTION 6 — THE OTHER SIDE (1.5 minutes)
The perspective nobody wants to hear. The uncomfortable truth, the nuance that gets lost in viral rage.

SECTION 7 — THE BIGGER PICTURE (1 minute)
What does this story reveal about our society, our technology, our values right now?

SECTION 8 — WHAT HAPPENS NEXT (1 minute)
The consequences. Legal, social, political, personal. Where is this story heading?

SECTION 9 — THE REAL LESSON (30 seconds)
What should we actually take away from this — beyond the hot take?

SECTION 10 — OUTRO + CTA (15 seconds)
